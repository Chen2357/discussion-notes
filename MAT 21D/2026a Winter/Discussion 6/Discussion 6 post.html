In Discussion 5, we talked about the associated quantities of a curve (velocity, tangent vector, curvature, and normal vector) and line integrals of scalar functions. The notes also introduces line integrals of vector fields and includes an exercise on it.

I skipped the solution for the second exercise (the one after warm-up) in the interest of time, but it contains an important lesson. The lesson is that the unit tangent vector (up to sign), curvature, and principal normal vector are all intrinsic to the shape of the curve, so they do not depend on the particular parametrization of the curve.

The velocity and speed, on the other hand, do depend on the particular parametrization of the curve.
